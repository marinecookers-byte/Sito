Heritage

Crafted where Italy meets the sea

Since 1974 — Conegliano, Inox Valley, Veneto

01 — Origin

Born where hills meet the Adriatic

Techimpex - Marine Cookers embodies the finest Italian tradition in the design and manufacture of cookers for sailing yachts and prestigious vessels. Our headquarters are in Conegliano, at the foot of the Venetian hills — a land celebrated for wine, cuisine, and manufacturing excellence.

From a territory so deeply connected to culinary culture, it was only natural that a company specialised in yacht cookers would be born here. And who better than Italians — undisputed masters of gastronomy — to design instruments for cooking at sea?

"Fifty years of Italian craftsmanship — forged in steel, refined by the sea." Marine Cookers — Techimpex, since 1974

02 — Inox Valley

Steel, craft and legacy

Founded in the early 1970s, Techimpex has drawn on the technical resources and artisanal expertise of the Inox Valley — a region renowned for stainless steel manufacturing, home to legendary names such as Zoppas and Zanussi.

This technical heritage, combined with a love of cooking, a passion for the sea, and unparalleled know-how, makes our cookers reliable instruments — refined down to the smallest detail.

03 — La Serenissima

Where Venice set sail

Our cultural roots are tied to the maritime tradition of the Serenissima Republic of Venice — one of history's most influential naval powers. Just as Venice united culture, technique and mastery of the seas, we unite refined design and technical reliability to create unique cookers for those who love the sea.

We work with some of Italy's most distinguished designers, including Marco Zito, professor and former director of IUAV Venice — one of Italy's most prestigious design institutes.

"From the lagoon of Venice to the open ocean — our cookers follow the sea." La Serenissima heritage

04 — Innovation

Firsts that defined the sector

A meeting with Luca Bassani, founder of Wally, inspired the first electric designer nautical cooker for sailing yachts — created at a time when modern design had not yet touched the aesthetics of contemporary sailing vessels. This became HORIZON4, still an icon of the sector.

Always for Wally, we developed the first fully aluminium lightweight cooker. And for Perini Navi, we engineered a special cooker with a unique reverse-gimballing system — a world first.

05 — Our Clients

Trusted by the world's finest yards

Our cookers have been trusted for decades by the world's most important shipyards, renowned distributors, and private owners. From modern Solaris yachts to classic vessels like William Fife III's Latifa (1936), from Giovanni Soldini's Elmo's Fire to Charles Leclerc's Ferretti.

A tradition that continues today with Cantiere del Pardo, ICE Yachts, Sunseeker, Sanlorenzo, and many others — wherever excellence at sea demands excellence in the galley.

01 — A Pioneer
The Italian company that changed marine cooking

Marine Cookers — Techimpex was the first Italian manufacturer to launch a complete collection of radiant electric cookers for vessels on the European market. Founded in Conegliano in 1974, the company pioneered a new category of marine appliances at a time when galley design was still largely dictated by gas technology. That bold move established a legacy of innovation that continues to define the brand today.

02 — Radiant & Induction
From radiant plates to full induction — always first

Building on the success of its radiant electric line, Marine Cookers — Techimpex went on to introduce the first complete induction cooker collection purpose-built for the marine environment. Each transition — from gas to radiant, from radiant to induction — was driven not by trend but by a commitment to delivering cleaner, safer, and more efficient cooking solutions aboard sailing yachts and motor vessels alike.

03 — Aesthetic Rigor
Class, restraint, and technical sophistication

Every electric cooker in this collection is defined by a rigorous aesthetic standard. Clean geometry, refined materials, and meticulous proportions place these appliances at the intersection of industrial design and artisanal craft. The result is a product that belongs — visually and functionally — in the galleys of the world's most demanding naval architecture firms and the most discerning private owners.

04 — Handcrafted Excellence
Meticulous craftsmanship, one unit at a time

Each unit is assembled by hand in Conegliano, in the heart of the Treviso industrial district — a region with a deep-rooted tradition in precision metalwork and mechanical craftsmanship. Marine-grade stainless steel, corrosion-resistant finishes, and components engineered for the harshest sea conditions reflect a manufacturing philosophy where performance and durability are never compromised.

"Trusted by Ferretti, Sunseeker, Sanlorenzo, and Cantiere del Pardo — because when excellence is the standard, the galley cannot be an afterthought."
